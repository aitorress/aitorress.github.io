# Research: AI Task Unbundling Evidence (2024-2025)

**Date:** 2026-01-27

## Summary

Current evidence shows AI is already unbundling knowledge work, with middle management emerging as an early pressure point. Radiology provides a nuanced case: AI handles specific tasks (screening mammograms) at expert-level accuracy but radiologists remain essential for complex interpretation. The "task unbundling" framing is now explicitly used by workforce researchers and companies restructuring their organizations.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Middle Management: First Major Category Under Pressure

**The trend is real and accelerating:**
- Middle management job postings dropped 40%+ between April 2022 and October 2024
- Microsoft publicly stated it wants "flatter team structure with more engineers and fewer middle managers"
- Intel removing multiple layers of team leadership, citing automation tools for performance tracking and coordination

**Major layoffs explicitly targeting middle management:**
- **Amazon:** 14,000 corporate jobs (~4% of white-collar workforce) to "reduce bureaucracy" and "remove organizational layers"
- **TCS (Tata Consultancy):** ~12,000 jobs, primarily targeting middle and senior management
- **Salesforce:** 4,000 customer support roles, claiming "AI can do 50% of the work"
- **Klarna:** Reduced staff by 40% through aggressive AI adoption

**Scale of 2025 layoffs:**
- October 2025: 153,000 job cuts—worst October for layoffs in 20+ years
- Year-to-date 2025: 946,000 job cuts (65% increase from 2024)
- 17,000+ explicitly attributed to AI, 20,000+ to "automation/technological updates"

- **Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/10/29/amazon-layoffs-ai-middle-managers-robots-factory-workers/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/22/tech-layoffs-2025-list/)
- **Credibility:** High — direct company announcements, Challenger Gray & Christmas data
- **How to use:** This directly supports the draft's claim that "middle management gets squeezed first." The mechanism is exactly what the draft describes: coordination, information aggregation, and report synthesis are AI-friendly tasks.

**The counterpoint:**
> "Middle management isn't just a structural layer—it's the organization's connective tissue. Those same managers are the ones translating strategy, coaching new leaders, and managing the exceptions that no algorithm handles well."

Yale's Budget Lab also notes that despite headlines, "U.S. labor has actually been little disrupted by AI automation since ChatGPT." The displacement may be overstated—or may just be early.

### 2. Radiology: The Task Unbundling Test Case

**Current adoption:**
- 48% of radiologists already using AI tools in clinical practice (2024 ESR survey)
- Another 25% planning to adopt soon
- Usage highest in large hospitals and teleradiology groups

**Task-level automation evidence:**
- Swedish trial: AI reduced radiologist workloads by 44% on screening mammograms while maintaining accuracy
- KMC Manipal Hospital (India): AI-enabled CT workflows serve 20-30 more patients daily
- AI handles: automated scheduling, assisted report generation, image quality checks, drafting reports from chest X-rays

**But it's augmentation, not replacement:**
> "AI is meant to aid radiologists, not to replace human intelligence in the reading room. Radiologists will continue to review all cases and findings."

> "The goal is an expert radiologist partnering with a transparent and explainable AI system. Together, they're better than either alone."

**The economic concern:**
> "Most productivity gains will go to employers, vendors, and private-equity firms. History shows automation boosts efficiency while reducing labour's share of income."

85% of radiologists believe AI will improve patient outcomes, but 63% worry about algorithmic bias and liability.

- **Source:** [RSNA](https://www.rsna.org/news/2025/january/role-of-ai-in-medical-imaging), [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s44401-025-00023-6), [Philips](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/news/archive/features/2025/ai-in-radiology-three-keys-to-real-world-impact.html)
- **Credibility:** High — peer-reviewed research, major medical imaging organizations
- **How to use:** Radiology is the clearest current example of task unbundling in a high-skill profession. The draft's "80% of economic value is scan-reading" claim appears unsupported—the reality is more nuanced. Tasks are being unbundled but the human role remains essential (for now).

### 3. Knowledge Work Unbundling: The Explicit Framework

**The term is now in use:**
> "Workforce task unbundling is a job-design approach that separates a role into discrete tasks and then assigns each task to automation, AI augmentation, or human work."

**Current state (2025):**
- 62% of organizations experimenting with AI agents in business functions
- Copilots handle drafts, summaries, and routine checks alongside people
- Microsoft's 2024 study found AI often acts as "coach, advisor, or teacher—gathering information and explaining it"

**The O*NET approach:**
Researchers use the Occupational Information Network (O*NET) to decompose jobs into 19,000+ task statements and assess which are automatable. This is the empirical version of what the draft describes intuitively.

**Projections:**
- By 2030: 30% of U.S. jobs could be fully automated
- 60% will see significant task-level changes
- PwC's 2025 AI Jobs Barometer: "AI can make people more valuable, not less—even in highly automatable jobs"
- Skill change is fastest in automatable jobs (up from 25% last year)

**What's harder to automate:**
- Jobs requiring physical work, human connection, hands-on skills
- Personal services, healthcare roles (nurses, therapists)
- "Human judgment under ambiguity—areas tied to context, ethics, and creativity"

- **Source:** [Microsoft New Future of Work Report 2025](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/New-Future-Of-Work-Report-2025.pdf), [PwC AI Jobs Barometer](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/issues/artificial-intelligence/ai-jobs-barometer.html), [Harvard Data Science Review](https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/ppjz2dg9/release/2)
- **Credibility:** High — major research institutions, large-scale data analysis
- **How to use:** This validates the draft's core framework. The language of "task unbundling" is now mainstream in workforce research. The draft is not speculating—it's describing what's already happening.

## Counterpoints and Complications

1. **Causation vs. correlation in layoffs.** Companies citing AI as the reason may be using it as cover for other restructuring. CNBC: critics say AI is "a good excuse."

2. **Implementation challenges are real.** 77% of organizations rate their data quality as average or poor for AI readiness. 95% faced data challenges during AI implementation. The transition is messier than projections suggest.

3. **Who captures the value matters.** Even if jobs survive, productivity gains may flow to capital, not labor. The draft should address this—"job survives" isn't the same as "workers are fine."

4. **Skill change is accelerating.** If the task bundle shifts faster than workers can retrain, the "jobs reorganize" story becomes a policy crisis.

---

## Gaps in Research

- No solid data on what percentage of radiologist economic value is "scan reading" (draft's 80% claim unsupported)
- Limited longitudinal studies on workers whose task bundles changed—are they doing better or worse?
- Need more data on retraining timelines: how fast can mid-career workers actually adapt?
