This is my ultimate mac and cheese recipe. It's so decadent, and you can have so much fun with the cheeses. My go-to is four different types: a base cheese (usually a nice cheddar, either sharp or regular), one for depth (smoked gouda is my go-to, but gruyere works amazing), a creamy one for richness (raclette, fontina, jarlsberg), and lastly, and this is a hard requirement, American cheese. In the video they finally got Blue Smoke to give up the secret to their sauce, and it was American cheese all along. Have fun with the cheeses, this is just a template, by no means a must. The video tops the whole thing with a Thanksgiving stuffing crust; I skip that part.

## Ingredients

For the infused cream:

- A touch of oil
- 1 garlic clove, smashed
- 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
- About 10 whole peppercorns
- A few slices of pickled jalapeños (fresh work too)
- 1 tablespoon of the pickled jalapeño vinegar (or white vinegar)
- 1/4 cup white wine
- Heavy cream

For the cheese, a pound to a pound and a quarter total, split however you like across:

- A base cheese: a nice cheddar, sharp or regular
- One for depth: smoked gouda (my go-to) or gruyere
- A creamy one for richness: raclette, fontina, or jarlsberg
- American cheese, about 6 slices (hard requirement)

For the rest:

- A box of cavatappi (shells are great too, they catch the sauce in little pockets)
- Panko bread crumbs
- Olive oil
- Salt

## Steps

1. Infuse the cream. In a small pot with a touch of oil, sweat the smashed garlic clove and the sliced onion for a minute or two. Add the peppercorns and the pickled jalapeños and cook another minute or two. Add the tablespoon of jalapeño vinegar and the white wine, and reduce until there's almost no liquid left. Pour in the cream, bring it to a gentle simmer, then kill the heat and let it sit and infuse for a few minutes. This is what quietly elevates the whole sauce.

2. Grate all the cheeses into a heat-proof bowl. This is where you play: keep the total around a pound to a pound and a quarter and split it across the four types however you feel like that day.

3. Make the double boiler. Fill a pot about a quarter of the way with water and bring it to a simmer, then set the bowl of cheese on top. Strain the infused cream into the cheese, pressing the solids to squeeze all that flavor out, and stir over the steam until it melts into a silky, luscious cheese sauce. The gentle heat is the point, no broken sauce here. Season with a touch of salt if needed.

4. Par-cook the pasta. Boil it in well-salted water for about 7 to 8 minutes, pulling it 3 to 4 minutes before it's actually done, a little past halfway. It finishes cooking in the oven, so do not cook it all the way.

5. Toss a handful of panko with a drizzle of olive oil so it can crisp up.

6. Add the par-cooked pasta to the cheese sauce and mix until every piece is glistening, then pour it into a baking dish. Scatter the panko over the top, but don't cover it completely. You want little peaks of noodle showing through.

7. Bake at 400°F for about 20 minutes, but don't go by time. Go by color, the crispness of the top, and bubbling around the edges. Bubbling is always a good sign. Done right, the pasta sticks to the fork with a little jiggle, and that's why you par-cooked it.
